Formalizing Mathematics

The Vision

This project aims to bridge rigorous mathematical formalization with the reasoning power of large language models. We use Lean, a powerful interactive theorem prover, to formalize mathematical structures and proofs. At the same time, we develop systems that empower LLMs to understand, explore, and construct formal proofs step-by-step using knowledge graphs, vector databases, and intelligent tactic selection.

What We’re Building

  • A structured knowledge base of Lean tactics with detailed explanations and examples.
  • A graph-based system to model dependencies between mathematical concepts and tactics.
  • An LLM-driven proof assistant that queries the knowledge base and navigates proof states intelligently.
  • A workflow that enables rapid collaboration between human insight and machine automation.

Why This Matters

Formal mathematics ensures complete rigor and verifiability, and LLMs offer scalable reasoning and exploration. Together, they could revolutionize how we approach deep problems in math, logic, and theoretical computer science— enabling discoveries, collaboration, and understanding at an unprecedented scale.
